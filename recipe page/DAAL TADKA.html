DAAL TADKA

Description

Dal Tadka is a popular Indian lentil dish made by cooking yellow lentils until soft and flavorful, then finishing them with a tempering (tadka) of ghee, garlic, cumin, and spices. It is comforting, nutritious, and packed with rich flavors.

This dish is commonly served with steamed rice, jeera rice, or roti. It is an everyday favorite in Indian households and is both easy to prepare and highly satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (or yellow lentils)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Wash the lentils thoroughly and pressure cook them with water and turmeric until soft.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add garlic, ginger, and green chilies, then sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
  5. Mix in the chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  6. Pour the cooked dal into the pan and stir well. Simmer for 5–10 minutes.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala and mix gently.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.
